The meshteq.ai platform

A focused analytics layer that sits on top of your existing sensors, historians and maintenance systems – built for operators who manage real assets, not just dashboards.

Unified data model

Normalise data from sensors, historians, CMMS, inspections and manual logs into a single asset-centric view, using your existing asset hierarchy.

  • Equipment & system hierarchy
  • Events, work orders & failures
  • Time-series signals & alarms

Predictive & prescriptive analytics

Apply anomaly detection, reliability models and domain rules to prioritise where attention delivers the highest risk and cost impact.

  • Early-warning anomaly scores
  • Bad actor & repeat failure analysis
  • Risk-based prioritisation

Operational decision support

Turn analytics into actions: track alerts, recommendations and executed work to close the loop between prediction and performance.

  • Alert triage & status tracking
  • Link to work orders & actions
  • Value and impact reporting

Designed for real operations

meshteq.ai focuses on a small number of high-value, high-clarity use cases to deliver measurable impact quickly.

Predictive maintenance

Monitor critical rotating equipment, pumps, compressors and utilities to detect abnormal conditions early and reduce unplanned failures.

  • Condition-based triggers & anomaly scores
  • Failure mode & bad-actor analysis
  • MTBF and downtime performance tracking

Production deferment analytics

Quantify and analyse unscheduled deferment, link to root causes and identify the interventions with the highest recovery potential.

  • Deferment tagging by asset & cause
  • Trend and benchmark vs. targets
  • Scenario and what-if views

Leak & loss monitoring

Combine IoT sensing with analytics to detect leaks and abnormal consumption in pipelines, facilities and utility networks.

  • Zone and DMA-level monitoring
  • Threshold & pattern-based detection
  • Integration with field inspections

How meshteq.ai fits into your landscape

  1. Ingest. Connect to historians, CMMS exports, inspection logs or IoT gateways.
  2. Model. Apply anomaly detection, reliability and domain models to your assets.
  3. Act. Prioritise alerts, assign actions and link to work orders.
  4. Validate. Track impact on deferment, reliability and cost over time.
